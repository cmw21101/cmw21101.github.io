A Visionary Stream of Conscious: Cognitive Neuroscience

This section is dedicated to the fied of Cognitive Nueroscience, specifically, memory. There are a few different types of memory we utilize. Sensory, short term, and long term memory. Sensory memory is the short-lived retention of sensory information, measureable in milliseconds to seconds. Short term memory is the retention of information over seconds to minutes. Just as there are subdirectories of directories pertaining to computer science, there are subdirectories of directories (short term and long term memories) pertaining to memory. A "subdirectory" of short term memory is working memory. Working memory is the retention of task-relevant information lasting seconds to minutes. This information can be engaged by sensory memory or long term memory, meaning it has the potential to become long term or sensory memory. Long term memory is the retention of memory over a long span of time (hours to years.) Two subdirectories of long term memory is declarative and nondeclaritive memory. Declarative memory is knowledge to which we have conscious access. Nondeclaritive memory is knowledge we typically have no conscious awareness of, like motor and cognitive skills.
